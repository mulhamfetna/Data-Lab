01 The gap

Every course teaches you to build data. Almost none teach you to judge it.

We surveyed ~35 platforms — paid coding schools, free hands-on sites, and executive programs. They split into three camps, and each one structurally shuts out the leader who won't write code.

Hands-on = coding

Learn by writing Python

DataCamp, Kaggle, Dataquest, Google. Genuinely interactive — but you must write code. A wall for a non-programmer.

$14–35/mo · chapter 1 free
Leader-framed = passive

Watch a video, take a quiz

Tableau, Qlik, "AI for Everyone", MIT, Wharton. Right audience — but you watch; you never touch a dataset and see it lie.

free video → or $3k–$10k exec-ed
Free = shallow or gated

A sampler, then a paywall

Free tiers stop at chapter 1; audit windows shrink to 1–2 weeks; the interesting parts want an account or a card.

signup · credit-card walls

No one lets a non-coder manipulate real data and models — for free, with no login, in Arabic. That is exactly what Data-Lab does.

05 Open by design

Take it. Fork it. Improve it. Just keep it open — and keep the credit.

Built to serve the greater good — free, hands-on data literacy for underserved science and engineering communities, in Arabic and English.

The licence does the work: AGPL-3.0 plus a Zenodo DOI mean anyone may run, fork, and build on Data-Lab — but every derivative must stay open and cite the author. Maximally free and maximally attributed, at once.
